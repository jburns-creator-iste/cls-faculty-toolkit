Section 01
Copy Guidelines

Apply these to every post, comment, caption, and email.

GuidelineWhat it means
Spell out full names on first referenceCustom Learning Services, not CLS. ISTE+ASCD, not the acronym alone.
Lead with the problem, not the solutionThe district's tension or challenge comes first. Custom Learning Services comes second.
Write from a partnership lens"We build alongside your team" over "our program offers." Focus on relationship, not product.
Write in your own practitioner voicePosts should sound like a thoughtful expert in the field, not a brand announcement.
Be specific, not general"40 teachers in Southfield" lands harder than "districts across the country."
One idea per postDon't combine an awareness angle and a consultation ask in the same post. Pick one.
Lead with the idea, not a questionState your observation or point of view directly. Avoid rhetorical openers.
Limit em dashesUse a comma, a period, or rewrite the sentence instead.
Avoid exclamation pointsB2B copy stays confident, not excited.
Write "Edtech," not "EdTech"Lowercase t, always.
State the idea directlySkip openers that delay the point. Get there in the first sentence.
Avoid AI-pattern copy constructionsSkip "Whether you are X or Y," three-part buzzword lists, and the false pivot structure. Write like a person.
Avoid the false pivotNever write "Not because X, but because Y" or "It's not about X — it's about Y." These are the most recognizable AI prose patterns and read as synthetic immediately.
Avoid these specific constructions"Here's the thing:" / "And that's the point." / "That's the work." / "This is what X actually looks like." / "Less X, more Y" / "The question isn't X. It's Y." / "What if X wasn't about Y?" — all of these signal AI-generated copy. Rewrite any sentence that uses them.
The CTA is always a conversationPoint to iste-ascd.org/custom (awareness) or iste-ascd.org/cls-contact (consultation). Never a hard sell.
Pre-service CTAs invite engagement, not clicksEnd pre-service posts with a question that invites replies — "Is anyone else seeing this?" or "What are you navigating in your district?" — not a product link.

Appendix A
Exemplars & Reference

Polished examples and a good/bad reference. Use these as benchmarks for tone, length, and structure.

Good Post vs. Avoid This

Write this

"Spent the morning with 40 teachers who were frustrated by AI, not excited about it. That is exactly where good implementation starts."
"The districts that see lasting change are not the ones that attended the most workshops. They are the ones that committed to the work over time."
"One question kept coming up in our session today: how do you build teacher confidence before you introduce new tools? That question is the whole ballgame."

Avoid this

"So excited to be at ISTE! What a transformational experience empowering educators with practical AI resources!"
"Join us at ISTE and discover the practical frameworks that will empower your team to take learning to the next level!"
"In today's rapidly changing world, educators need resources and practical strategies to join the movement and empower their students."
Social Post Exemplars
LinkedIn · Awareness
Most districts start with the wrong question. They ask: "Which AI tools should we adopt?" The better question is: "What do our teachers need to feel confident enough to try?" Confidence is not a mindset intervention. It is a design problem. Teachers do not resist technology because they do not understand it. They resist it when adoption threatens their professional identity and no one has built a bridge. That bridge is what sustained professional learning builds. Not a one-day workshop on prompting. A structured, supported progression from awareness to application to fluency. ISTE+ASCD's Custom Learning Services works with districts on exactly that progression. iste-ascd.org/custom
~130 words. No hard CTA. Leads with the tension that resonates most with instructional leaders.
LinkedIn · Lead Generation
Four years ago, Southfield Public Schools was rebuilding. Not from a crisis. From a decision to stop relying on one-off trainings and to start building the kind of instructional capacity that lasts. Four years later, they are Academic State Champions with a 91% graduation rate. That is what a sustained partnership looks like in practice. If your district is at that decision point right now, I would welcome a 15-minute conversation. iste-ascd.org/cls-contact
~90 words. Leads with proof, ends with a low-friction CTA.
X · On-Site
On-site in [City] today working with a team of 35 teachers on AI readiness. The question that came up more than any other: "How do I explain this to parents?" That is not a communications problem. It is a trust problem. And it is exactly the right place to start. iste-ascd.org/custom
~65 words. Real, specific, in-the-moment. The observation does the work.
LinkedIn · Post-Professional Development
Wrapped up a three-day engagement with [District Name] last week. When we started, the conversation was about tools. By day two, it had shifted entirely to trust: trust between teachers and administrators, trust in the change process, trust in their own professional judgment. That shift is what makes everything else possible. You cannot install it. You have to build it. If your district is starting that conversation, our team at ISTE+ASCD would be glad to be part of it. iste-ascd.org/cls-contact
~105 words. Replace [District Name] if you have permission to use it.
